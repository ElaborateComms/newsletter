International Seafarers’ Day 2025 — My Harassment Free Ship

Every year on June 25, we celebrate International Seafarers’ Day — honouring your hard work and the important role you play in world trade.

This year’s topic is My Harassment Free Ship. Harassment and bullying that takes place on board is a serious problem making life at sea difficult and unpleasant for many seafarers.

What Is harassment?

Harassment means bad or unfair behaviour from a person or a group of people; a colleague, supervisor, or even a passenger, which makes you feel disrespected, worried or stressed.

Harassment can be:

  • • Unpleasant comments or gestures
  • • Unwanted touching
  • • Unfair treatment
  • • Cyber-harassment through messages or social media
  • • Sexual harassment or abuse of power

Harassment can affect your happiness, your health, and your ability to do your job safely.

How You Can Help Stop Harassment

Whilst companies are responsible for making sure there are clear rules and training, each seafarer can do something to make their workplace a kinder and more respectful place:

Speak up: If you feel comfortable, tell the person their behaviour is not acceptable. Often, this can make them realise their actions are hurting someone.

Write down details: Keep a record of what happened — the time, place and people involved — in case you need to report it later.

Seek help: Talk to a colleague you trust or your company’s person who handles complaints. Some organisations also provide confidential helplines (like ISWAN’s SeafarerHelp) for support and guidance.

Respect and be kind: Always treat your colleagues with kindness and fairness.

Look after each other: Support your friends and colleagues if you see they are treated unfairly. We’re all in the same team.

Safety Focus: Preparing for the summer season

As the summer season approaches, it’s important for all our seafarers to stay vigilant and well-prepared for the challenges that come with warmer weather and increased activity at sea.

Key points to remember:
  • • Hydration and heat management: High temperatures can quickly lead to dehydration, heat exhaustion or heatstroke. Always ensure you drink sufficient water throughout your shift, take regular breaks in shaded or air-conditioned areas when possible and wear lightweight, breathable clothing.
  • • Safe deck work: Increased heat can make deck surfaces extremely hot and slippery. Always wear appropriate footwear, use gloves when handling metal equipment and be cautious to avoid burns or slips.
  • • Health and wellbeing: Summer brings a higher risk of fatigue due to long daylight hours and intense work schedules. Prioritise good rest, maintain a balanced diet and monitor your energy levels. If feeling unwell, inform your supervisor immediately.
  • • Equipment and Fire Safety Checks: The risk of fire incidents can rise during hotter months. Ensure all firefighting equipment is checked regularly, ventilation systems are working properly and any flammable materials are stored correctly.

At Danica the safety and well-being of our crew is always our top priority. We count on your professionalism and awareness to ensure a safe and productive summer season. Let’s all stay sharp, take care of ourselves and each other, and keep safety at the heart of every operation. Safe sailing!
